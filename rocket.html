Rocket Flight Trajectory Simulator

MATLAB (BASE, NO TOOLBOXES) · 1-DOF FLIGHT DYNAMICS · BARROWMAN STABILITY · OPENROCKET VALIDATION

A 1-DOF simulator of a rocket's full vertical flight — launch, powered ascent, burnout, coast, apogee, and parachute recovery — with a static-stability check, a motor-selection study, and validation against OpenRocket.

The physics

The rocket is a point mass moving vertically. At each instant the simulator computes the net force and integrates the resulting acceleration with ode45 to track altitude, velocity, and mass over the flight.

m(t)·a = T(t) − m(t)·g − ½·ρ(h)·Cd·A·v²·sign(v)

Around that core: a thrust-curve model integrated to total impulse and mapped to a motor class, an exponential atmosphere where air density falls with altitude, velocity-opposing drag with separate coefficients for ascent and parachute descent, and a two-phase integration that deploys the chute after apogee, using ODE event detection to pin apogee and landing exactly. It also reports a Barrowman static-stability estimate (center of pressure versus center of gravity, as static margin in calibers) and max-Q for structural load reference. A separate study sweeps several motors to find the best fit for a target apogee.

Validation against OpenRocket

I rebuilt the same rocket in OpenRocket with an AeroTech H165R motor and compared, with total impulse matched to the real motor.

Simulator vs. OpenRocket
MetricThis simOpenRocketΔ
Apogee (m)542445+21.7%
Max velocity (m/s)109103+5.6%
Max acceleration (g)19.412.8+50.9%
Total impulse (N·s)165161+2.5%

Max velocity and total impulse match closely. Apogee and peak acceleration run consistently higher, and the discrepancy traces cleanly to the model's simplifications: a fixed drag coefficient (versus OpenRocket's Mach-dependent drag, which lets my rocket coast higher) and a synthetic thrust curve that front-loads the burn more than the real motor does.

Limitations and next steps

1-DOF only, so no wind or off-vertical launch; fixed rather than Mach-dependent drag; a synthetic thrust curve where real .eng motor data would improve the acceleration match; and a stability estimate using an approximated CG that real CAD mass properties would make exact.
