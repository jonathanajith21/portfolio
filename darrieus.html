Darrieus VAWT — Design & DMST Analysis

FREECAD · MATLAB · DOUBLE MULTIPLE STREAMTUBE THEORY · QBLADE CROSS-CHECK

A two-blade curved-blade Darrieus wind turbine, designed in FreeCAD and analyzed with DMST theory. The central finding is a negative result, established carefully: this rotor's solidity is too high for DMST to produce valid answers — confirmed by two independent solvers failing the same way.

The story

I designed the rotor in FreeCAD back in my senior year of high school, then revisited it to predict its performance. I first wrote a DMST solver from scratch in base MATLAB to learn the method — upwind/downwind streamtube coupling, induction-factor iteration, NACA 0018 polar interpolation over the full angle-of-attack range, torque integration. It diverged to non-physical power coefficients. On its own, that could simply have been my coding error. So I rebuilt the analysis in QBlade, an open-source, validated VAWT tool from TU Berlin, and ran it on the same rotor. It diverged in exactly the same way.

Why the method fails here

DMST models a VAWT as flow through two actuator disks in series, an upwind and a downwind half. For this rotor, the upwind half extracts so much momentum that the downwind streamtube balance has no valid solution: the induction factor saturates at 1.0 across the downwind pass, and the predicted power coefficient goes strongly negative. Lowering the relaxation factor and raising the iteration count did not resolve it; both solvers show identical behavior. Low-solidity VAWTs where DMST works well sit around 0.1–0.2, with ~0.3–0.4 the documented onset of streamtube breakdown. At ~0.45, this rotor is past it.

What I take from it

The reproduction is the result. Because an independent, validated tool failed identically, the cause is the rotor and the method, not the implementation — and the right next step is a higher-fidelity approach (a vortex model or CFD) that doesn't rely on the streamtube momentum balance, or a lower-solidity redesign with a smaller chord or larger radius.
