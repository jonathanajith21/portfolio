CLeadership & Involvement

Kenya Project — Engineers Without Borders, UMass Amherst

2025 – PRESENT
CLEAN WATER INFRASTRUCTURE · IMALE, KENYA · EWB-USA

For the past two semesters I've been part of the Kenya project team in the UMass Amherst chapter of Engineers Without Borders, a student-run organization that partners with communities to build sustainable, community-driven engineering solutions. The Kenya program works with the village of Imale on access to clean water: with a borehole already drilled, the team is now working toward the design and installation of a solar-powered pump system to bring water up from deep underground, so the community has a reliable supply even through the driest months. The work runs through weekly project meetings, design reviews with EWB-USA, and coordination with mentors and local partners in Kenya.
