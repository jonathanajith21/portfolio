Engineering Intern — Envac

JUN – AUG 2025 · PNEUMATIC WASTE COLLECTION SYSTEMS · SOLIDWORKS · SITE OPERATIONS

Envac builds automated pneumatic waste collection systems: waste dropped at an inlet is transported at high speed through underground pipe networks to a central collection station, replacing truck-based collection entirely.

What I did

Working alongside the engineering team, I shadowed engineers during site visits to see how inlets, pipe networks, and collection stations operate together as an integrated system in a live installation. I reviewed technical documentation and system schematics to understand how the fans, valves, separators, and control systems coordinate during a collection cycle, and assisted with routine inspection and maintenance observations, noting how components such as discharge valves and pipe joints are checked and serviced.

I also helped compile and organize operational data, collection cycle logs and waste volume figures, summarizing findings for the team, and sat in on meetings and design reviews to see how engineering decisions are actually made, including the recurring challenge of retrofitting these systems into existing buildings.

Final project — Mini PWCS in SolidWorks

For my final project I designed a miniature pneumatic waste collection system, modeling each component from scratch: a waste inlet hatch, a tapering drop chute, a curved transport pipe, a cyclone separator, and a collection bin. The parts were built with a range of SolidWorks features, extrudes, lofts, sweeps, revolves, shelling, and fillets, applying the company's drawing standards to keep the work consistent with professional practice. The components were then mated into a full assembly along a shared central axis, representing the path waste follows through the system.
